General AI Hot News

Anthropic

Claude Cowork Goes GA — Enterprise Features & Managed Agents in Beta

As of April 9, Claude Cowork is generally available on macOS and Windows. Enterprise additions include role-based access controls, group spend limits, usage analytics, expanded OpenTelemetry support, a Zoom MCP connector, and per-tool connector controls. Separately, Claude Managed Agents launched in public beta — a fully managed agent harness with secure sandboxing and SSE streaming. Computer use also landed in the Claude Code CLI this week, letting agents click through native desktop UIs and fix what breaks — all from the terminal.

GPT-5.5 "Spud" Enters Final Stretch — 78% Odds of April Release

GPT-5.4 has now fully rolled out across ChatGPT, Codex, and the API, with GPT-5.3 Instant Mini becoming the new fallback model for rate-limited users. Google

Gemini 3.1 Ultra — 2M Token Context, 750M Users, Leading Benchmarks

Google's Gemini platform now has 750 million monthly active users. Gemini 3.1 Ultra ships with a 2-million token context window and native multimodal reasoning across text, image, audio, and video. It posts 77.1% on ARC-AGI-2 and leads 13 of 16 major benchmarks. For enterprise AI strategy conversations with clients, Gemini's cost-per-performance advantage is now a serious talking point.

NVIDIA

GTC 2026 — Rubin Architecture Revealed, Agentic AI Takes Centre Stage

NVIDIA's GTC 2026 in San Jose marked a pivot from benchmark theatre to real enterprise deployments. The Rubin GPU architecture was unveiled, promising another generational leap in training and inference. Agentic AI sessions drew the largest attendance of the conference — a signal that the industry is now in the deployment phase, not the hype phase. Enterprise customers are asking "how do we run agents at scale?" not "what is an agent?"

DeepSeek

DeepSeek V4 Confirmed — 1T Parameters, R2 Successor Faces Delays

DeepSeek V4 specs are confirmed: 1 trillion parameters, 81% on SWE-bench, priced at $0.30/MTok — a 90% cost reduction vs GPT-5.4. However, Reuters reports that DeepSeek's R2 reasoning model successor faces delays due to performance issues and export-control chip constraints.
