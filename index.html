ServiceNow Ships a Genuinely Agentic Onboarding Workflow: a Team of AI Agents Drafts the Ramp-Up Plan, the Manager Keeps Two Checkpoints
NEW ServiceNow Agentic
Now Assist for HRSD now ships a “Generate Onboarding Ramp-Up Plan” agentic workflow that triggers automatically when a new hire is five or fewer days from their start date — no manual kick-off. A team of AI agents pulls from the onboarding journey record, HR cases, team plans, user and manager profiles and job details, extracts the new hire’s existing skills from their resume, cross-references the job description and interview notes to find skill gaps, recommends learning and catalog items, then assembles a structured multi-stage plan. The detail that matters is governance: the manager stays in the loop at exactly two checkpoints — draft review and final publish — while everything in between is handled by the agents. For a Deloitte HRSD practitioner this is the demoable artefact clients keep asking for: agentic HR that does real work but has human-in-the-loop control built into the product, not bolted on at audit time. Build it on a PDI this week and you have a concrete answer to “show me safe agentic HR” — and a ready-made template for the conversation about where the human checkpoints belong.
