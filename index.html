HR & ServiceNow Intelligence

EmployeeWorks Goes GA — Moveworks + ServiceNow Now Serving 200M Employees

🆕 GA ServiceNow

ServiceNow officially launched EmployeeWorks alongside the "Autonomous Workforce" initiative. The product wires Moveworks' conversational AI and deep enterprise search directly into ServiceNow's unified portal, turning natural-language intent into governed, multi-system execution. The first Autonomous Workforce specialist — an L1 Service Desk AI Specialist — is in controlled availability now, with GA expected Q2 2026. Shares rose 5% on the announcement. Moveworks also remains available as a standalone product for orgs not yet on ServiceNow.

→ ServiceNow Newsroom

HRSD March 2026 Store Release: Voice Agent V3.0, Team Digest & Offboarding Suite

HRSD Store Drop

The March store release for Now Assist for HRSD packs three major updates. Voice Agent V3.0 adds 8 new languages (German, Spanish, French, Japanese, Brazilian Portuguese, French Canadian, Chinese Mandarin, Dutch) with improved context retention — a huge win for global enterprise rollouts. Team Digest on Manager Hub (v4.8) uses Now Assist (now compatible with all four LLM providers) to surface Growth Experience, feedback, journeys, and task data in a single AI-generated summary with workflow triggers. A new Offboarding & Alumni Experience module rounds out the release, smoothing departing-employee transitions and enabling ongoing alumni engagement.

→ ServiceNow Community Deep Dive

ServiceNow: The "AI Control Tower" Narrative & $600M GenAI ACV Milestone

Agentic AI ServiceNow

ServiceNow is now actively positioning the Now Platform as the central AI Control Tower for enterprise orchestration — coordinating agents across IT, HR, finance, and customer service through a single governance layer. The Pro Plus SKUs with specialised GenAI capabilities have exceeded $600M in annual contract value, validating the platform's commercial traction. AI Agent Studio lets builders create custom agents via natural language, with pre-built agents across ITSM, CSM, HRSD, and SecOps available out of the box.

→ ServiceNow AI Control Tower Analysis

Workday + Sana: A Bold New Agentic AI Strategy for HR

Hot Take HR AI

Josh Bersin's deep-dive on the Workday–Sana partnership signals Workday's answer to ServiceNow's EmployeeWorks: an AI front-end that brings agentic HR capabilities (similar to Joule or MS Copilot) natively into the Workday ecosystem. The Workday Frontline Agent (expected spring 2026) will handle shift swaps, hour limits, and staffing changes — early adopters report up to 90% reduction in manager time spent. Workday is also integrating Paradox's Conversational ATS for frontline hiring acceleration. The race to "agentify" HCM is now three-way: ServiceNow/EmployeeWorks, Workday/Sana, and SAP/Joule.

→ Josh Bersin Analysis

SAP Joule + Microsoft Copilot Integration Deepens — a2a & MCP Interop Live

SAP Interop

SAP announced that Joule now fully interoperates with Microsoft Copilot through the a2a and MCP protocols — users can access SAP SuccessFactors workflows and Microsoft 365 productivity in a single context-switched experience. Joule is on track to become an expert HR/leadership assistant powered by Galileo in 2026, making it a compelling alternative front-end for SuccessFactors HRSD deployments. For consultants straddling ServiceNow and SAP, this interop layer is worth watching closely.

→ SAP Joule Update (Bersin)
General AI Hot News

OpenAI Drops GPT-5.4 — Pro & Thinking Variants, 1M Context, 83% GDPval

🔥 Major Drop OpenAI

OpenAI released GPT-5.4 on 5 March in three flavours: standard, GPT-5.4 Thinking (reasoning), and GPT-5.4 Pro (high performance). The model consolidates previous separate stacks — coding from GPT-5.3-Codex, improved reasoning, and agentic computer-use capabilities — into a single frontier model. API context window extends to 1M tokens. Benchmark highlights: record scores on OSWorld-Verified and WebArena Verified computer-use tests, 83% on GDPval (knowledge work), claims 33% fewer false individual claims vs GPT-5.2. Legacy GPT-5.1 models are being retired, with the deep research mode sunset on 26 March.

→ TechCrunch Coverage

NVIDIA GTC 2026: Vera Rubin, $1T Orders & NemoClaw Enterprise Preview

NVIDIA GTC 2026

NVIDIA's GTC 2026 (16–19 March, San Jose) was headlined by Jensen Huang declaring "the inference inflection has arrived." Blackwell + Vera Rubin purchase orders through 2027 are expected to hit $1 trillion. Vera Rubin, rolling out later in 2026, delivers 10× performance per watt vs Grace Blackwell; Google Cloud and Azure are among the first to deploy NVL72 rack-scale systems. Most relevant for builders: NemoClaw — NVIDIA's open-source reference stack for running enterprise OpenClaw agents — entered early preview, bundling OpenShell runtime and NVIDIA Agent Toolkit. Also notable: the Groq 3 LPU (from NVIDIA's $20B Groq acquisition) ships Q3 2026.

→ NVIDIA GTC 2026 Live Updates

Anthropic: Claude Sonnet 4.6 with 1M Context Beta + Opus 4.6 Coding Boost

Anthropic Update

Anthropic has shipped Claude Sonnet 4.6 — upgraded across coding, computer use, long-context reasoning, agent planning, and design — with a 1M token context window now in beta. Opus 4.6 received a focused coding skills uplift. New platform capabilities include the thinking.display: "omitted" flag for faster streaming of extended thinking, and an updated Models API exposing max_input_tokens, max_tokens, and a capabilities object. Community speculation around Claude 5 (codenamed "Fennec" for Sonnet 5) is building after an apparent Vertex AI log sighting — no official confirmation yet.

→ Claude Platform Release Notes

Google Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite: Fastest, Cheapest Model — $0.25/M Tokens

Google 3 Mar 2026

Google launched Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite on 3 March — the cheapest model in the Gemini family at $0.25 per million input tokens and $1.50 output. Generation speed is 45% faster than Gemini 2.5 Flash, with a 2.5× reduction in time-to-first-token. Supports 1M token multimodal context. Tops six of the standard benchmarks against GPT-5 mini and Claude 4.5 Haiku. Available via Vertex AI and Google AI Studio. For HR chatbot or document-processing workloads where cost matters, this is the new baseline to beat.

→ Google Blog: Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite

OpenClaw Ecosystem Explodes: NemoClaw Preview, PicoClaw v0.2.3, Nanobot v0.1.4

Open Source Agent Frameworks

The OpenClaw ecosystem (launched 30 Jan 2026) continues its rapid proliferation. Highlights this week: NVIDIA's NemoClaw entered early preview (16 March), providing an enterprise-ready reference stack on top of OpenClaw. PicoClaw v0.2.3 (17 March) added Windows/Linux system tray UI, sub-agent status tracking, cron security gates, and 2 security fixes. Nanobot v0.1.4 (HKUDS's ultra-lightweight OpenClaw implementation) added multi-provider web search, LangSmith integration, VolcEngine support, and Telegram reply context. Over a dozen serious variants now exist — NanoClaw, ZeroClaw, Moltis, PicoClaw, Nanobot, NullClaw — each targeting different enterprise weaknesses.

→ OpenClaw Ecosystem Digest #10

DeepSeek "V4 Lite" Spotted; V4 Multimodal Still Pending Official Launch

DeepSeek Watch

DeepSeek's long-awaited V4 multimodal model (picture, video, and text generation) has missed multiple launch windows (Feb, Lunar New Year, early March). On 9 March, Chinese tech media spotted an unofficial update on DeepSeek's website with expanded context handling — community members are calling it "V4 Lite," though DeepSeek has not officially confirmed. V4 is being optimised for Huawei and Cambricon chips (MIT-licensed, deployable locally). Once it drops, expect immediate community benchmarking against GPT-5.4 and Gemini 3.1.

→ DeepSeek V4 Analysis

xAI Grok 4.20 Beta + $20B Series E; Deepfake Controversy Clouds Launch

⚠️ Watch xAI

xAI closed a $20B Series E and launched Grok 4.20 Beta and a Multi-agent Beta via the Enterprise API, along with video generation and a revamped image generation pipeline.
